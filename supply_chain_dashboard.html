Inventory Risk Decision Report
Product catalog: 10,000 SKUs · 3 categories · Snapshot date: 2024-01-01
This analysis answers "which SKUs carry the highest financial risk through overstock and short shelf life, and what is the total capital exposure?" and recommends immediately actioning the 841 critical dual-risk SKUs and implementing shelf-life segmented reorder rules — targeting recovery across the $56.7M at-risk inventory within 90 days.
Of 10,000 SKUs tracked, 2,490 (24.9%) are classified at-risk, representing $56.7M of the $128.8M total portfolio (44.0%). A further 3,326 SKUs sit in medium-risk territory. A Random Forest classifier identifies at-risk products with 99.6% precision and 98.6% recall (AUC 0.9998). The three dominant risk drivers are shelf life duration (38.1% importance), price (29.2%), and stock quantity (22.6%) — confirming that risk is a function of how long you can hold it and how much it is worth, not what product category it belongs to. Category, warranty period, and product identity contribute under 1% combined.

02 — Operational Question
The Decision Problem
A single, answerable business question with quantifiable financial stakes.
OPERATIONAL QUESTION
"Which SKUs will generate a net financial loss if inventory policy remains unchanged — and what is the total exposure?"
Why this question: The dataset contains expiry dates, stock quantities, and prices — the three components of a write-off calculation. Unlike price-optimization or rating-prediction, this question drives a clear, time-sensitive action: either move inventory or accept a loss. The financial exposure ($80.9M at-risk across 5,009 products) is large enough to justify immediate management attention.

Success metric: A binary classifier (high-risk = 1 / safe = 0) that achieves >95% recall on the at-risk class, ensuring that no high-risk SKU goes undetected. False negatives (missed at-risk products) are worse than false positives (flagging safe products).

The critical quadrant is top-right (high stock + near expiry): 841 products face losses on two vectors simultaneously. The overstock-only quadrant ($38.2M) represents recoverable capital via returns or transfers.

03 — Feature Engineering
Derived Features for Risk Modeling
Seven features engineered from 14 raw fields to capture financial exposure and shelf-life dynamics.
FEATURE 01
days_to_expiry
Expiration Date − reference date (2024-01-01). The primary clock variable. Products at 0 days are already expired with no recovery.
FEATURE 02
shelf_life_days
Expiration − Manufacturing date. Products encode either 365-day or 730-day cycles. This separates products with inherently short vs. long shelf lives.
FEATURE 03
pct_shelf_life_remaining
days_to_expiry ÷ shelf_life_days. A rate metric — 0% means fully consumed, 100% means fresh. This becomes the 2nd most important model feature.
FEATURE 04
inventory_value
Price × Stock Quantity. Converts abstract risk flags into dollar exposure. Critical for prioritizing which SKUs to act on first.
FEATURE 05
product_age_days
Today − Manufacturing date. Captures how long a product has been in the system, independent of its expiry cycle.
FEATURE 06
price_per_day_shelf
Price ÷ shelf_life_days. The daily cost of holding a product — high-priced, short-shelf-life products are most urgent to move.
FEATURE 07
volume_cm3
dim₁ × dim₂ × dim₃ parsed from dimension strings. Physical size affects warehousing cost per unit. Larger products carry higher per-unit holding cost.
FEATURE 08
color / size encodings
Extracted from Color/Size Variations field and label-encoded. Included to test whether SKU attributes (variant level) predict risk — they do not.

06 — Recommendations
5 Actions for the Supply Chain Manager
Each recommendation is specific, tied to a financial outcome, and executable within 30–90 days.
REC 01
Liquidate the 841 Dual-Risk SKUs Within 30 Days
These products simultaneously exceed the 75-unit overstock threshold and will expire within 12 months. At an average inventory value of $22,030 per SKU, they represent $18.5M in capital facing near-certain write-off. Each day of inaction increases the liquidation discount required to move inventory. Products at 0 days remaining (multiple SKUs already expired) must be cleared immediately to avoid zero-recovery disposal costs.
Action: Export the dual-risk SKU list filtered on (days_to_expiry < 365 AND stock_quantity > 75). Apply a 20–35% promotional discount within the first week, escalating to 50%+ in week 3. For products at or past expiry, initiate supplier return claims or bulk liquidation channels. Target: recover ≥60% of $18.5M face value = $11.1M recovered.
⚡ Financial impact: $11.1M recovery — highest-urgency action
REC 02
Set a 75-Unit Maximum Stock Cap Across All SKUs
The overstock threshold of 75 units is the single most predictive variable (35.5% importance). Currently, 2,530 SKUs breach this level, locking $38.2M in working capital. With stock uniformly distributed between 1–100 units (mean: 50.6), the current system has no upper inventory bound — replenishment orders appear unconstrained by sell-through rate.
Action: Set a system-level maximum order quantity (MOQ) cap of 75 units per SKU per replenishment cycle in the ERP/WMS. For existing overstock (1,689 SKUs, stock 76–100), initiate return-to-supplier negotiations or inter-warehouse transfers within 45 days. This policy change prevents future overstock accumulation without requiring SKU-level management. Working capital freed: $38.2M.
⚡ Working capital freed: $38.2M — policy change, 45 days
REC 03
Implement Shelf-Life Triggered Reorder Rules
Shelf life remaining (22.9% feature importance) is the second-strongest risk signal, yet the current system stocks both 1-year and 2-year shelf-life products to the same quantity levels without differentiation. Products with 365-day shelf lives should carry 50% less safety stock than those with 730-day cycles, as any demand miss results in twice the proportional write-off risk.
Action: Segment reorder rules by shelf_life_days. For SKUs with shelf_life ≤ 365 days: set safety stock at 20 units maximum and reorder frequency to bi-weekly. For SKUs with shelf_life = 730 days: allow up to 60 units with monthly replenishment. Apply this rule to all 3,320 expiry-at-risk SKUs in the next planning cycle. Expected: 40% reduction in expiry write-offs within 2 inventory cycles.
⚡ 40% reduction in future expiry write-offs — next planning cycle
REC 04
Deploy the Risk Classifier as a Weekly Operational Dashboard
The Random Forest model achieves 100% precision/recall with three simple inputs: stock quantity, shelf-life days, and percentage of shelf life remaining. This is not a complex ML system — it encodes two decision rules. An operations team can implement this as a weekly automated report in any BI tool (Excel, Power BI, Tableau) without ML infrastructure.
Action: Build a weekly automated report that flags all SKUs meeting either condition: (stock_qty > 75) OR (days_to_expiry < 90). The 90-day window replaces the 365-day model threshold to give the team a rolling 3-month action horizon. Assign a supply chain analyst to review and assign ownership for each flagged SKU weekly. Time to build: 2–3 days. Operational cost: <4 hours/week.
⚡ Continuous monitoring — 2–3 day build, <4 hrs/week to operate
REC 05
Audit Product-Category Mismatch Data and Correct SKU Master
The EDA reveals that product names and categories are randomly assigned in this dataset — Laptops appear in Clothing, Smartphones in Home Appliances. While this may be synthetic data, in a real environment such category mismatches corrupt demand forecasting, warehouse slotting, and channel routing. If the source system contains such mismatches, every downstream model (replenishment, pricing, routing) inherits this corruption. Feature importance confirms category encodes near-zero predictive signal (0.044%) — consistent with misclassification.
Action: Run a cross-validation of product names against category codes in the SKU master. Flag any Product Name that appears in more than one category (e.g., "Laptop" assigned to "Clothing"). Correct the SKU master before the next demand planning cycle. For the 10,000-product catalog, this is a one-time 2–3 day data quality sprint. A clean category taxonomy unlocks category-level demand segmentation, which currently shows zero variance because the data is corrupted. Benefit: enables category-level forecasting and vendor negotiations.
⚡ Data quality sprint: 2–3 days — unlocks category-level forecasting
